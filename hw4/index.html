Project Overview

Through this homework, we embarked on a journey to develop a sophisticated path tracer capable of producing photorealistic images. We began by optimizing the rendering process with a Bounding Volume Hierarchy (BVH), significantly improving efficiency in handling complex scenes by streamlining ray-object intersection tests. Progressing further, we tackled the simulation of direct illumination, crafting lighting models that accurately depicted the influence of light emanating directly from sources. Our exploration deepened as we introduced global illumination to our repertoire, enabling us to capture the intricate dance of light as it bounces across multiple surfaces, thereby injecting our scenes with lifelike lighting effects, such as color bleeding and soft shadows. Utilizing Monte Carlo methods, we adeptly handled diffuse reflection sampling and implemented strategies like Russian Roulette for unbiased path termination, striking a delicate balance between rendering performance and image quality. Through these endeavors, we gained a profound understanding of key concepts in photorealistic rendering, including spatial data structures, material behavior under light, and complex light transport models. By the conclusion of the homework, we successfully rendered complex 3D scenes with detailed lighting and shadows, showcasing our advanced skills in creating visually stunning and realistic images.

Part 2

Describe the effects of changing the spring constant ks; how does the cloth behave from start to rest with a very low ks? A high ks?

  • A lower ks makes the cloth more flexible and less resistant to stretching. When subjected to gravity, the cloth stretches more and exhibits a more fluid-like behavior. It takes longer to settle into a resting state because the springs are less forceful in pulling the cloth back to its original shape.
  • A higher ks results in a stiffer cloth. The springs are very resistant to stretching, causing the cloth to retain its shape more rigidly against external forces. The cloth settles into a resting state more quickly, as the strong springs rapidly counteract any displacements.

Above: with default parameters.

Above: Low ks (1) and High ks (100000).

What about for density?

  • A lower density makes the cloth lighter. It settles more slowly under gravity alone, as there is less force pulling it down. The cloth is also less stretched out as the gravitational force acting on it is smaller (it weighs less).
  • Increasing the density makes the cloth heavier so it is more stretched out. Heavier cloth also falls and settles faster due to the greater force of gravity acting on it.

Above: Low density (1) and High density (100000).

What about for damping?

  • Low damping means that there is less resistance to motion changes, leading to more prolonged oscillations once the cloth is released. The cloth continues to swing and ripple for a longer time before settling.
  • High damping adds resistance to motion changes, causing the cloth to quickly lose any introduced energy. As a result, movements are more muted, and once the cloth comes to its final place, it does not swing anymore. This makes the cloth appear heavy and less responsive to forces.

Above: Low damping (0%) and High damping (1%).
